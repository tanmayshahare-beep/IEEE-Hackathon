1. TOMATOES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Major Growing States:
  Maharashtra, Bihar, Karnataka, Uttar Pradesh, Orissa, Andhra Pradesh,
  Madhya Pradesh, West Bengal, Assam.

Climate Requirements:
  - Warm-season crop.
  - Optimal temperature: 21–27°C.
  - Temperatures above 32°C reduce fruit set; below 10°C harms growth.

Soil Requirements:
  - Well-drained sandy loam or red loam, rich in organic matter.
  - pH: 5.5 – 7.0.
  - Avoid waterlogged or heavy clay soils.

Planting / Transplanting Seasons:
  - Kharif (monsoon): June – July
  - Rabi (winter): November – December
  - Summer: February

Harvesting Periods:
  - Kharif: October – November
  - Rabi: April – June
  - Summer: April – September

Economics (Per Acre):
  - Investment (seeds, fertilizer, labour, irrigation): ₹60,000 – ₹1,25,000
    (average ~₹65,000, up to ₹90,000 in some regions)
  - Yield: 10 – 20 tonnes (100–200 quintals)
  - Revenue:
      • Favourable market price (₹14–19/kg): ₹1,40,000 – ₹3,80,000
      • Distress price (₹2–5/kg): ₹20,000 – ₹50,000 (often below cost)
  - Note: Prices are highly volatile. Current market (March 2026) shows severe
    price crashes with farmers receiving as low as ₹2–6 per kg in wholesale
    markets, making profitability uncertain.

Additional Notes:
  - Grown year-round due to multiple seasons.
  - Water stress during flowering and fruit development harms yield.
  - Calcium deficiency can cause blossom-end rot.


2. GRAPES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Major Growing Regions & Zones:
  (1) Hot Tropical: Maharashtra (Nashik, Pune), Northern Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh
      – accounts for ~70% of production.
  (2) Mild Tropical: Tamil Nadu, Southern Karnataka (Bangalore).
  (3) Sub-tropical: North India (Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh).

Climate Requirements:
  - Hot and dry climate; high humidity encourages fungal diseases.
  - Temperature range: 15–40°C depending on variety and growth stage.
  - Regions follow distinct pruning/harvesting patterns.

Soil Requirements:
  - Well-drained soil.
  - Rootstocks like Dogridge used to manage soil salinity.

Planting (Pruning & Growth Initiation):
  - Main crop: pruning in September – November; harvest ~3–4 months later.
  - Early crop: pruning in July – August for an October – November harvest.

Harvesting Periods:
  - Main harvest: December – April
  - Early harvest: October – November
  - Wine grapes: February – March

Economics (Per Acre):
  - Specific per‑acre cost and revenue data were not available in the current
    search results. Economics vary widely by state (Maharashtra, Karnataka,
    Tamil Nadu), variety (table vs. wine grapes), and farming practices
    (drip irrigation, trellising, etc.).
  - Grapes are generally a high‑value crop with significant export potential,
    but initial establishment costs can be substantial.

Additional Notes:
  - Drip irrigation is common; water stress reduces quality.
  - “Double pruning and single harvest” system used in tropical regions.


3. APPLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Traditional Growing Regions:
  - Himachal Pradesh
  - Jammu & Kashmir
  - Uttarakhand
  Altitude: 1500 – 2700 m (temperate climate).

Non-Traditional / Experimental Regions:
  - Maharashtra (Kolhapur, Solapur)
  - Karnataka (Mangaluru)
  - Telangana

Climate Requirements:
  - Temperate crop; requires cool winters with 1000–1600 chill hours.
  - Summer temperatures: 15–25°C.
  - Non‑traditional regions use heat‑tolerant varieties and controlled irrigation.

Soil Requirements:
  - Well-drained soil; loamy with good organic matter.

Planting:
  - Traditional: trees planted during winter dormancy (perennial crop, no fixed
    annual planting like vegetables).
  - Non‑traditional: pruning is used to manage growth; e.g., in Telangana,
    pruning in December leads to harvest in May.

Harvesting Periods:
  - Traditional Himalayan regions: July – October
  - Non‑traditional regions (e.g., Telangana, Maharashtra): May (depending on
    variety and pruning schedule)

Economics (Per Acre):
  Traditional Orchards:
    - Yield: 4 – 5 tonnes per acre (10–12 tonnes per hectare)
    - Time to first harvest: up to 10 years
    - Market price: ₹60 – ₹220 per kg depending on grade and location
        • Haryana markets: ₹6,000–7,500 per quintal (₹60–75/kg)
        • Tamil Nadu markets: ₹11,000–22,000 per quintal (₹110–220/kg)

  High‑Density Orchards:
    - Yield: 20 – 28 tonnes per acre (50–70 tonnes per hectare)
    - Fruits by second year
    - Price: ₹100–140 per kg (almost double traditional varieties)
    - Government subsidies: up to 50% in Jammu & Kashmir for switching to
      high‑density systems

  Note: Profitability in apples depends heavily on variety, orchard management,
  and market access. Non‑traditional regions using low‑chill varieties are
  emerging but have different cost structures.

Additional Notes:
  - Climate change is shifting traditional apple belts.
  - Farmers in warmer areas are successfully growing low‑chill varieties
    (e.g., ‘Anna’, ‘G‑9’) with techniques like regulated irrigation to
    simulate winter dormancy.
